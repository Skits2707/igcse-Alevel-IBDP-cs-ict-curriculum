Section 03 — My Practice

My Practice

These aren't just Computer Science stories. They're evidence of who I am as an educator.

Story 01

The Girl Nobody Saw

A shy student who hid in the classroom, barely spoke, and had no confidence. Her parents and teachers had given up on her. I stopped trying to 'teach' her and started trying to know her. Built trust slowly. At six, she became one of the top students in her class — now seven, she attends one of Hong Kong's top schools.

Relationship precedes learning. You cannot reach a child you do not see.
Story 02

The Boy Everyone Gave Up On

A student with severe behavioural needs, removed from the classroom for three years. Labelled 'unteachable'. I simply refused to give up on him. Six months of consistent expectations and patience. He can now sit, listen, participate, and complete work independently. He wrote a story on his own — beaming with pride.

'Unteachable' usually means 'not yet reached'. Every child has a door to dignity.

Technology & AI Literacy

Honest. Not Fearful.

My Background

I hold a Level 8 Honours Bachelor's in Cybercrime and IT Security from SETU Ireland. My formal training is in complex systems. My passion is making them accessible to young learners.

How I Teach AI

Kids are growing up in the age of AI. I don't pretend it doesn't exist or warn them that screens will ruin their eyes. I'm honest: AI can help you if you know how to use it. Screen time has limits, but small doses with purpose beat blind prohibition.

Section 06 — Bilingual Practice

Bilingual Practice

EAL scaffolding in technical subjects. Every student deserves access to the language of Computer Science.

Teaching Computer Science in a bilingual context isn't just translation — it's about building conceptual bridges. Every technical term is a potential barrier for EAL learners. My approach removes those barriers systematically.

My Approach

Bilingual Keywords First — Every lesson opens with 3–5 key terms displayed in English and Chinese. Students see the vocabulary before they encounter the concept, priming comprehension.

Visual Scaffolding — Diagrams, flowcharts, and physical demonstrations reduce language dependency. A student can understand binary addition by moving physical cards before reading a single sentence.

Sentence Starters — For extended written answers, I provide sentence starters in both languages. This removes the 'blank page' anxiety and lets students focus on demonstrating technical understanding.

Collaborative Learning

Pair Before Individual — Students discuss concepts in pairs (often mixing language abilities) before individual work. This builds confidence and generates peer explanations that are sometimes more effective than the teacher's.

Bilingual Glossary Preview — Every programme includes a comprehensive glossary with English, Chinese characters, and Pinyin. Students reference these throughout the course.

Section 08 — The Curriculum

Four Integrated Programmes

One seamless pathway from first digital steps to university-level computer science

🖥️

IGCSE Computer Science

CIE 0478

Theory foundations and programming fundamentals. Two-year programme with Paper 1 (Theory) and Paper 2 (Problem Solving & Programming).

  • Binary, hardware, networks, security
  • Python programming & pseudocode
  • 5 mock exams with gap analysis
📱

ICT Programme

CIE 0417 + Common Core

Digital literacy to advanced productivity. Nine-year spiral from Grade 2 through IGCSE ICT — word processing, spreadsheets, databases, web design.

  • Age-appropriate digital citizenship
  • Microsoft Office / Google Workspace
  • HTML, CSS & database skills
🎓

A-Level Computer Science

CIE 9618

Advanced theory, programming project, and examination mastery. AS + A2 covering databases, Boolean logic, AI, and practical software development.

  • SQL, Boolean algebra, floating-point
  • Programming project (NEA-style)
  • University & career pathways
🌍

IB DP Computer Science

IB DP (2024+)

SL and HL pathways with Internal Assessment support. Topics 1–7, TOK connections, case study preparation, and IA handbook with exemplars.

  • SL & HL differentiation
  • Complete IA guide with criteria
  • TOK & university pathway links
